Theoretical Project

Task Management Application

  • My Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 4 Months

Project Description

Taskly is a theoretical task management application I design for a UX course. The scope of the project was to research and analyze the project management software market, conduct user research, and design a minimum viable product version of the Taskly application.

My Role

Competitor analysis, user testing, prototyping, visual design

Approach

Competitor Analysis

One of the first steps was to evalute the current market. Here's a subset of the information I collected of the competitors I looked at:

Basecamp 3
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Basecamp has calendars and chat rolled in, so the need for external applications to manage projects should be minimized. They also don't charge per user; pricing packages are based on business type.

  • Opportunities:

    Taskly could allow for task time estimates and have a “burn down” instead of stopping at task due dates. Also allow tasks to be assigned to more than one person.

Asana
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Asana is free or very affordable depending on team size.

  • Opportunities:

    Could make their marketing site more informative of features and less "fluffy". Navigation within the actual app could be more intuitive.

Active Collab
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Financial tools built into the application for small businesses. Ability to set hourly rates and track time on tasks, has invoicing feature.

  • Opportunities:

    In order to customize Taskly to its target market, perhaps it could offer a handful of extra features on top of the basic functionality that get added to the app a la carte? This could be the cost determiner and allow teams to get and pay for only the features they need. This lets Taskly also be a niche product without having to actually specialize too much.
