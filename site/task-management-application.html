Theoretical Project

Task Management Application

  • My Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 4 Months
Project Reflections

I created Taskly for a class project in early 2016. It was my first UX project after working for three years as a full stack developer.

While my UX skills are stronger today, I wanted to include this project as a benchmark of my progress as a designer.

Some things I would do differently if I did this project today:

  • I'd find a stronger differentiator for Taskly as a business model. At the time, I didn't have the experience to fully understand why this was so important. Now, identifying what makes Taskly different would be a top priority
  • I would do more sketching and experimenting with a bunch of different approaches, instead of iterating on one idea
  • I'd use Axure over Photoshop for my designs. The combination of Photoshop and Invision here probably quadrupled my work with little, if any, benefit
  • I would code the style guide (UI Kit) and give more examples of repeatable components
  • If this were a real project, detailed interaction specs would be essential

Approach

Competitor Analysis

One of the first steps was to evalute the current market. To do this, I analyzed three popular competitors:

Basecamp 3
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Basecamp has calendars and chat rolled in, so the need for external applications to manage projects should be minimized. They also don't charge per user; pricing packages are based on business type.

  • Opportunities:

    Taskly could allow for task time estimates and have a “burn down” instead of stopping at task due dates. Also allow tasks to be assigned to more than one person.

Asana
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Asana is free or very affordable depending on team size.

  • Opportunities:

    Could make their marketing site more informative of features and less "fluffy". Navigation within the actual app could be more intuitive.

Active Collab
  • Competitive Advantage:

    Financial tools built into the application for small businesses. Ability to set hourly rates and track time on tasks, has invoicing feature.

  • Opportunities:

    Taskly could offer optional tools for users to customize their workspace and only pay for the features they need.

Lean Canvas

User Interviews & Surveys

Two research methods were used to understand user needs prior to starting any designs. The first method was a survey, which had a total of 33 participants. The survey asked users what project management software they had experience with and what the pro's and con's were of each. The results of the survey were formatted into an affinity diagram to identify patterns and themes.

To gain qualitative data, the second method was to interview three potentional users. Interviewees were asked to expand on concepts uncovered by the survey.

Sketches & Wireframes

Per the research results, three key features were emphasized in the prototyping phase: task management, chat and calendars. Simple prototypes were first drafted on paper, then tested with users. The insights gained from these user tests were applied to create simple digital prototypes that were later tested using InVision.
